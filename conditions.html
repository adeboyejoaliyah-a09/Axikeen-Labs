Mental Health Conditions A-Z

A comprehensive guide to understanding various mental health conditions. Find information and resources for each condition below.

A B C D E G H I M N O P S T

A

ADHD (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder)

A neurodevelopmental disorder affecting attention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Agoraphobia

Fear of places and situations that might cause panic, helplessness, or embarrassment.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Anorexia Nervosa

An eating disorder characterized by weight loss (or lack of appropriate weight gain in growing children); difficulties maintaining an appropriate body weight working for height, age, and stature; and, in many individuals, distorted body image.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Antisocial Personality Disorder

A mental health condition in which a person consistently shows no regard for right and wrong and ignores the rights and feelings of others.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Anxiety Disorders

A group of mental disorders characterized by significant feelings of anxiety and fear.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

A developmental disorder that affects communication and behavior.

Symptoms Management How to Help

B

Binge Eating Disorder

A severe, life-threatening, and treatable eating disorder characterized by recurrent episodes of eating large quantities of food.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Bipolar Disorder

A mental health condition that causes extreme mood swings that include emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression).

Symptoms Management How to Help

Body Dysmorphic Disorder

A mental health disorder in which you can't stop thinking about one or more perceived defects or flaws in your appearance.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD)

A mental health disorder that impacts the way you think and feel about yourself and others.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Bulimia Nervosa

A serious, potentially life-threatening eating disorder characterized by a cycle of bingeing and compensatory behaviors such as self-induced vomiting.

Symptoms Management How to Help

C

Conduct Disorder

A range of antisocial types of behavior displayed in childhood or adolescence.

Symptoms Management How to Help

D

Depression (Major Depressive Disorder)

A mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Dissociative Identity Disorder

A disorder characterized by the presence of two or more distinct personality states.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Disruptive Mood Dysregulation Disorder

A childhood condition of extreme irritability, anger, and frequent, intense temper outbursts.

Symptoms Management How to Help

E

Eating Disorders

A range of psychological disorders characterized by abnormal or disturbed eating habits.

Symptoms Management How to Help

G

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

Severe, ongoing anxiety that interferes with daily activities.

Symptoms Management How to Help

H

Hoarding Disorder

A persistent difficulty discarding or parting with possessions because of a perceived need to save them.

Symptoms Management How to Help

I

Insomnia Disorder

A common sleep disorder that can make it hard to fall asleep, hard to stay asleep, or cause you to wake up too early and not be able to get back to sleep.

Symptoms Management How to Help

M

Mixed episodes

A state during which features of mania and depression, such as agitation, anxiety, fatigue, guilt, impulsiveness, irritability, morbid or suicidal ideation, panic, paranoia, pressured speech and rage, occur simultaneously.

Symptoms Management How to Help

N

Narcissistic Personality Disorder

A mental condition in which people have an inflated sense of their own importance, a deep need for excessive attention and admiration, troubled relationships, and a lack of empathy for others.

Symptoms Management How to Help

O

OCD (Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder)

A pattern of unwanted thoughts and fears (obsessions) that lead you to do repetitive behaviors (compulsions).

Symptoms Management How to Help

Oppositional Defiant Disorder (ODD)

A condition in which a child displays an ongoing pattern of an angry or irritable mood, defiant or argumentative behavior, and vindictiveness toward people in authority.

Symptoms Management How to Help

P

Panic Disorder

An anxiety disorder characterized by reoccurring unexpected panic attacks.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Paranoid Personality Disorder

An eccentric personality disorder characterized by paranoia and a pervasive, long-standing suspiciousness and generalized mistrust of others.

Symptoms Management How to Help

PTSD (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder)

A disorder triggered by a terrifying event — either experiencing it or witnessing it.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Postpartum Depression

Depression that occurs after childbirth.

Symptoms Management How to Help

S

Schizoaffective Disorder

A mental health disorder that changes how you think, feel and act. It includes symptoms of schizophrenia, such as hallucinations or delusions, and symptoms of a mood disorder, such as depression or mania.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Schizophrenia

A serious mental disorder in which people interpret reality abnormally.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)

A type of depression that's related to changes in seasons.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Separation Anxiety Disorder

A childhood disorder characterized by anxiety that's excessive for the child's developmental level and related to separation from parents or others who have parental roles.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Social Anxiety Disorder

A chronic mental health condition in which social interactions cause irrational anxiety.

Symptoms Management How to Help

Substance Use Disorder

A disease that affects a person's brain and behavior and leads to an inability to control the use of a legal or illegal medication or drug.

Symptoms Management How to Help

T

Tourette Syndrome

A disorder that involves repetitive movements or unwanted sounds (tics) that can't be easily controlled.
