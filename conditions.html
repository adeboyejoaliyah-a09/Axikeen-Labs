Mental Health Conditions A-Z

A comprehensive guide to understanding various mental health conditions. Find information and resources for each condition below.

A B D G O P S

A

ADHD (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder)

A neurodevelopmental disorder affecting attention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity.

Anxiety Disorders

A group of mental disorders characterized by significant feelings of anxiety and fear.

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

A developmental disorder that affects communication and behavior.

B

Bipolar Disorder

A mental health condition that causes extreme mood swings that include emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression).

Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD)

A mental health disorder that impacts the way you think and feel about yourself and others.

D

Depression (Major Depressive Disorder)

A mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest.

Dissociative Disorders

Mental disorders that involve experiencing a disconnection and lack of continuity between thoughts, memories, surroundings, actions and identity.

G

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

Severe, ongoing anxiety that interferes with daily activities.

O

OCD (Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder)

A pattern of unwanted thoughts and fears (obsessions) that lead you to do repetitive behaviors (compulsions).

P

Panic Disorder

An anxiety disorder characterized by reoccurring unexpected panic attacks.

PTSD (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder)

A disorder triggered by a terrifying event — either experiencing it or witnessing it.

Postpartum Depression

Depression that occurs after childbirth.

S

Schizophrenia

A serious mental disorder in which people interpret reality abnormally.

Social Anxiety Disorder

A chronic mental health condition in which social interactions cause irrational anxiety.
