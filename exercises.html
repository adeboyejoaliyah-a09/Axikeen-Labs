Exercises for Wellness

Practical tools and techniques to help you manage specific challenges. Healing takes time, and these small steps can make a big difference.

Anxiety Relief
  • Deep Breathing: Inhale slowly for 4 seconds, hold for 4 seconds, and exhale for 6 seconds. Repeat 5 times.
  • Journaling: Set a timer for 10 minutes and write down everything on your mind without judgment.
  • Calming Music: Create a playlist of soft instrumental or nature sounds to help regulate your nervous system.
Coping with Depression
  • Morning Light: Try to get 20 minutes of sunlight or bright light within an hour of waking up.
  • Gratitude Practice: Write down 3 small things you are thankful for today (e.g., warm coffee, a comfortable bed).
  • Connect: Send a simple text or call one trusted friend or family member. Connection fights isolation.
Managing Stress
  • Mindfulness: Practice sitting in silence and observing your breath for 5–10 minutes.
  • Chunking Tasks: Break overwhelming to-do lists into tiny, manageable steps (micro-goals).
  • Movement Breaks: Take a 5-minute walk or stretch every hour during work/study.
  • Relaxation Ritual: Drink a warm cup of caffeine-free tea while focusing solely on the taste and warmth.
Better Sleep (Insomnia)
  • Digital Detox: Turn off all screens 1 hour before bed to reduce blue light exposure.
  • Bedtime Routine: Read a physical book or listen to a sleep story/meditation.
  • Consistency: Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day to set your circadian rhythm.
PTSD Support
  • Grounding 5-4-3-2-1: Name 5 things you see, 4 you touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.
  • Art Therapy: Use drawing, painting, or coloring to express emotions when words fail.
  • Gentle Yoga: Practice trauma-informed yoga stretches to release stored physical tension.
Autism & Sensory Support
  • Sensory Regulation: Use weighted blankets, stress balls, or noise-canceling headphones as needed.
  • Visual Structure: Use visual calendars or checklists to create predictable routines.
  • Nature Time: Spend quiet time in nature to reset sensory overload.
Personality Disorders (BPD/etc)
  • DBT Skills: Practice "Stop" skills—Stop, Take a step back, Observe, Proceed mindfully.
  • Mood Tracking: Keep a daily log of emotions and triggers to identify patterns.
  • Role-Playing: Practice difficult conversations in a safe space or mirror before having them.
